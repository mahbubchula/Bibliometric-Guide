TI Electric vehicle adoption in smart cities: A systematic review
SO TRANSPORTATION RESEARCH PART D-TRANSPORT AND ENVIRONMENT
LA English
DT Review
DE electric vehicles; smart cities; sustainable transportation; adoption barriers; policy
ID BATTERY ELECTRIC VEHICLES; CONSUMER ACCEPTANCE; CHARGING INFRASTRUCTURE; ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT; PURCHASE INTENTION
AB This paper provides a comprehensive systematic review of electric vehicle (EV) adoption in smart cities. We analyze 150 peer-reviewed articles published between 2015-2024 to identify key factors influencing EV adoption, including infrastructure development, government policies, consumer attitudes, and technological advancements. Our findings reveal that charging infrastructure availability and government incentives are the most significant drivers of EV adoption. We also identify research gaps and propose future research directions for sustainable urban mobility.

TI Autonomous vehicles and urban planning: Challenges and opportunities
SO JOURNAL OF TRANSPORT GEOGRAPHY
LA English
DT Article
DE autonomous vehicles; urban planning; land use; parking; traffic management
ID SELF-DRIVING CARS; AUTOMATED VEHICLES; CITY DESIGN; MOBILITY AS SERVICE; SHARED MOBILITY
AB Autonomous vehicles (AVs) will fundamentally transform urban planning and city design. This study examines the potential impacts of AV adoption on urban form, land use patterns, and transportation infrastructure. Through scenario analysis and expert interviews with 50 urban planners across 10 cities, we identify key planning challenges including parking demand reduction, roadway redesign, and pedestrian safety. Our findings suggest that AVs could free up to 25% of urban land currently dedicated to parking, offering unprecedented opportunities for urban redevelopment.

TI Public transport optimization using big data analytics: A case study
SO TRANSPORTATION RESEARCH RECORD
LA English
DT Article
DE public transport; big data; route optimization; passenger flow; smart cards
ID BUS RAPID TRANSIT; TRAVEL DEMAND; PASSENGER COUNTING; ORIGIN-DESTINATION; DATA MINING
AB This paper presents a novel approach to public transport optimization using big data analytics from smart card transactions. We analyze 10 million trip records from Bangkok's BTS Skytrain system to identify passenger flow patterns, peak hour demand, and route optimization opportunities. Our machine learning models achieve 92% accuracy in predicting passenger demand, enabling transit agencies to optimize service frequency and reduce operational costs by 15%. The methodology can be adapted to other transit systems worldwide.
